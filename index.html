Hello! I am a Ph.D. student in Computer Science at the University of Pittsburgh, where I am fortunate to be advised by Professor Jacob Biehl.

My research focuses on developing privacy-preserving sensing systems for shared spaces, with particular emphasis on balancing utility, privacy, and equity in human-computer interaction. I am interested in understanding how technology can better serve diverse populations while respecting individual privacy concerns.

I previously completed my B.S. in Computer Science at Cornell University in 2020, where I worked as a teaching assistant for AI and machine learning courses and gained research experience in the Smart Computer Interfaces for Future Interactions (SciFi) Lab.

Research Interests

My work spans human-computer interaction, privacy-preserving technologies, computer vision, and spatial computing. I am particularly interested in:

  • Privacy-preserving sensing systems for public and shared spaces
  • Understanding space use behavior through computational methods
  • Balancing utility, privacy, and equity in ubiquitous computing
  • Serious games for healthcare applications
